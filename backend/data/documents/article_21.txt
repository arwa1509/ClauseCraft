ARTICLE 21 – PROTECTION OF LIFE AND PERSONAL LIBERTY

No person shall be deprived of his life or personal liberty except according to procedure established by law.

The Supreme Court of India has interpreted Article 21 in a broad manner to include various rights essential to life and liberty. These include the right to live with human dignity, right to livelihood, right to privacy, right to health, and right to a fair trial.

Article 21 applies to both citizens and non-citizens. It acts as a safeguard against arbitrary actions of the State and ensures that any deprivation of life or liberty must follow due legal procedure.
